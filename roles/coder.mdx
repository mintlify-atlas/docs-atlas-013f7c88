Coders are Riwi bootcamp students who use Kairo to learn at their own pace. The platform analyzes your academic performance and soft skills to generate a personalized 4-week growth path, so you always know exactly where to focus.

## Account creation

You can create a Kairo account in two ways:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Email + OTP" icon="envelope">
    Register with your email address. Kairo sends a one-time password (OTP) to verify your email before activating your account.
  </Card>
  <Card title="OAuth" icon="key">
    Sign in with Google or GitHub. Kairo upserts your account automatically after the provider callback — no separate registration step required.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Email registration flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submit your details">
    `POST /api/auth/register` — provide your full name, email, and password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox">
    Kairo sends an OTP to your email address via SMTP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify your email">
    `POST /api/auth/verify-otp` — submit the code from your inbox. Your account is activated immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in">
    `POST /api/auth/login` — use your email and password. Kairo creates a session and sets the `riwi.sid` cookie.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If the OTP expires before you verify, use `POST /api/auth/resend-otp` to request a new one.
</Tip>

## Clans

Every coder belongs to one of three clans:

| Clan | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Turing** | Named after Alan Turing |
| **Tesla** | Named after Nikola Tesla |
| **McCarthy** | Named after John McCarthy |

Your clan is stored in the `clan_id` field of the `users` table and determines which Team Leader monitors your progress. You cannot change your clan yourself — contact your TL or an admin.

## First login: the onboarding assessment

The first time you log in, `first_login` is set to `true` in your account. Kairo intercepts every request to `/api/coder/*` and redirects you to complete the soft skills assessment before you can access the dashboard.

<Warning>
  You cannot skip the onboarding assessment. All coder routes are protected by the `checkOnboarding` middleware. Until you complete the assessment and `first_login` is set to `false`, every API call returns a `403 Onboarding Required` error with `redirect: "/onboarding"`.
</Warning>

The assessment is available at the `onboarding.html` view. It records the following scores on a 1–5 scale:

- **Autonomy** — how independently you work
- **Time management** — how well you plan your schedule
- **Problem solving** — how you approach obstacles
- **Communication** — how you express and receive information
- **Teamwork** — how you collaborate with others
- **Learning style** — `visual`, `auditory`, `kinesthetic`, or `mixed`

Once you submit the form, Node.js writes your scores to the `soft_skills_assessment` table and calls `PATCH /api/auth/complete-onboarding` to flip `first_login` to `false`. You are then redirected to your dashboard.

<Info>
  Team Leaders and admins bypass onboarding entirely — the `checkOnboarding` middleware only applies to coders.
</Info>

## Daily workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check your dashboard">
    Open `dashboard.html`. Your personalized focus cards are displayed here — six cards organized by priority (2 high, 2 medium, 2 low). Each card represents a topic where you need reinforcement, derived from your Moodle scores and soft skills assessment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Work through your daily activities">
    Open `assignment.html` to see today's activities. Activities come in three types:

    | Type | Description |
    |------|-------------|
    | `guided` | Step-by-step instructions with minimal decisions |
    | `semi_guided` | Partial structure — you fill in the gaps |
    | `autonomous` | Open-ended task with full creative freedom |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mark activities complete">
    When you finish an activity, call `PATCH /api/coder/activities/:id/complete`. You can include a `reflection_text` and the time you spent. This updates the `activity_progress` table.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit evidence">
    Upload a PDF or paste a URL as proof of your work. Evidence is stored in the `evidence_submissions` table and is visible to your TL.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the AI chat tutor">
    Open `IAtrainer.html` to chat with the AI tutor. The tutor answers questions in the context of your active learning plan.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check TL feedback">
    Watch for the notification bell indicator on any view. A red dot means your Team Leader has sent you new feedback (`is_read = false` in the `tl_feedback` table). Open the message and it clears automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Requesting a new plan

If you have completed your current plan or your situation has changed, you can request a fresh AI-generated plan:

```bash
POST /api/coder/plan/request
```

Kairo reads your latest `soft_skills_assessment` and `moodle_progress` records, forwards the data to the Python AI service, and saves a new set of six focus cards in `complementary_plans`.

## Generating practice exercises

Request an AI-generated exercise on any topic:

```bash
POST /api/coder/exercise/generate
```

After completing the exercise, submit your answer for evaluation:

```bash
POST /api/coder/exercise/:exerciseId/submit
```

You can review your past submissions at any time:

```bash
GET /api/coder/exercise/:exerciseId/submissions
```

## Searching learning resources

Kairo includes a RAG-powered resource search backed by the `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` embedding model. Use it to find PDFs and links your TL has uploaded:

```bash
POST /api/coder/resources/search
```

To list all resources available to your clan:

```bash
GET /api/coder/resources
```

To download a specific resource:

```bash
GET /api/coder/resource/:id/download
```
