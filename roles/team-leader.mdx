Team Leaders (TLs) are mentors responsible for tracking the progress of an entire clan of coders. Kairo gives you an analytics dashboard, AI-generated reports, and direct feedback tools so you can identify struggling coders before they fall behind.

## Account setup

TL accounts use the same authentication flow as coders â€” email + OTP or OAuth (Google/GitHub). The only difference is that your account is created with `role = 'tl'` in the `users` table.

<Info>
  TLs skip the onboarding assessment entirely. The `checkOnboarding` middleware checks for `role = 'tl'` and calls `next()` immediately â€” you are taken directly to your dashboard after your first login.
</Info>

All TL routes are protected by `isAuthenticated` followed by `hasRole('tl')`. Any request from a non-TL session receives a `403 Forbidden` response.

## Core responsibilities

### 1. Monitor the clan dashboard

Open `dashboard.html` to see a real-time overview of your clan's health. The dashboard queries the `v_coder_risk_analysis` view and surfaces:

- **Risk alerts** â€” coders automatically flagged as `high` or `medium` risk based on low `average_score` (below 70) or low `autonomy` score (2 or below)
- **Completion percentages** â€” derived from the `v_coder_dashboard` view
- **Clan rankings** â€” ordered by `average_score`, updated in real time

```bash
GET /api/tl/dashboard
```

### 2. Review coder progress and score history

Drill into any coder's score history to understand trends over time:

```bash
GET /api/tl/coder/:id/score
```

The response includes the full historical score record from `moodle_progress`, so you can spot whether a drop is recent or part of a longer pattern.

To see how your clan ranks overall:

```bash
GET /api/tl/ranking
```

### 3. Send feedback

Send weekly, activity-specific, or general feedback directly to a coder:

```bash
POST /api/tl/feedback
```

Feedback is stored in the `tl_feedback` table with `is_read = false`. The coder sees a notification bell indicator until they open the message.

Feedback types:

| Type | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| `weekly` | End-of-week summary of progress |
| `activity` | Targeted comment on a specific activity or submission |
| `general` | Catch-all for check-ins or encouragement |

### 4. Review and score evidence submissions

Coders upload evidence (PDF files or links) for their completed activities. You review and score each submission:

```bash
GET /api/tl/submissions
POST /api/tl/submissions/:id/review
```

Each review awards the coder **+15 points**, which feeds into their `average_score` and risk calculation. Consistent review keeps risk flags accurate.

### 5. Upload learning resources

Upload PDF resources for your clan to find via the semantic search:

```bash
POST /api/tl/resource/upload
```

<Warning>
  Files must be PDFs (`application/pdf`). The maximum file size is **20 MB**. Uploads above this limit are rejected by the multer middleware.
</Warning>

To manage your uploaded resources:

```bash
GET /api/tl/resource/list
DELETE /api/tl/resource/:resourceId
```

### 6. Generate AI reports

Request an AI-generated executive report for a single coder or your entire clan:

```bash
POST /api/ai/reports
```

Reports are stored in the `ai_reports` table with a `target_type` of `coder` or `clan`. Each report includes a `summary_text`, `risk_level`, and `recommendations` field.

## Risk alert workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify at-risk coders">
    On your dashboard, the `v_coder_risk_analysis` view flags coders as `high` risk when `autonomy â‰¤ 2` **and** `average_score < 70`, and as `medium` risk when either condition is true alone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Investigate score history">
    Call `GET /api/tl/coder/:id/score` to review the coder's score trend. Check whether the drop is isolated to one module or systemic.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate an AI report">
    Call `POST /api/ai/reports` with `target_type: "coder"` and the coder's ID. The AI service cross-references soft skills signals and Moodle scores to produce targeted recommendations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send targeted feedback">
    Call `POST /api/tl/feedback` with `feedback_type: "general"` or `"activity"` to address the specific issue. Keep the message actionable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key metrics

The following metrics appear throughout the TL dashboard and are used to calculate risk:

| Metric | Source | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| `completion_percentage` | `v_coder_dashboard` | Percentage of plan activities marked complete |
| `average_score` | `moodle_progress` | Academic score from Moodle, updated per module |
| `risk_level` | `v_coder_risk_analysis` / `risk_flags` | `low`, `medium`, or `high` |
| Clan ranking | `GET /api/tl/ranking` | Score-ordered list of coders in your clan |
